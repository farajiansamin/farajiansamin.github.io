Introduction

Tangible User Interfaces (TUIs) provide new possibilities for creative learning. Results showes that TUIs had many advantages, such as they (1) are novice-friendly, (2) support children's cognitive process and development(which helps them to remember and recall what thay have learned), (3) promote their initiatives, (4) enable them to think outside the box, and (5) encourage communication and collaboration in an authentic context [1].

A persona to represtent the target users

Sheet box is a tangible game that aims to help children ( 3-6 years old) to get familiar with musical notes and read music sheets. In this game, one big box called "Key" is the identifier, and children can hear the sound of each musical note by placing boxes at different distances to the "Key".

Some of the sketches from design process

The Key box here is the Clef in musicial sheets(A Clef is a musical symbol used to indicate which notes are represented by the lines and spaces on a musical stave) ,and it can be moved to different postions.This means that children can practice reading notes based on different Clifs by placing the note boxes in differenet position .
